Kimchi Fried Rice (Kimchi Bokkeumbap)

Description

It all started one fateful night in December of 1988.

The harsh winds of a cruel winter pelted my face with snow. I know I was with my family. I know they were trying to say something to me -- but they just couldn't. Their voices were not loud enough to pierce through the wind.

They say that snow makes everything seem quiet. Peaceful. I disagree. To me, there is nothing louder than the sound of silence, which brings me to my next point.

Kimchi fried rice. It's uh, exactly what it sounds like.

Ingredients

Measure accordingly to taste.

  • 1tbsp Canola Oil
  • 1/4 cup Ground Pork (Omit if vegetarian)
  • 1 sliced green onion, separate the white and green
  • 1 cup kimchi, drained and chopped
  • 1 tbsp or more of Gochujang
  • 3 cups of cooked rice (Please use a rice cooker.)
  • 1 tbsp Sesame Oil
  • 1 tbsp Butter
  • 1 Egg

Cooking Instructions

  1. Heat canola oil in large skillet over high heat. Stir ground beef and white parts of green onion into skillet and reduce heat to medium. Cook and stir beef and green onion until meat is browned and onion is fragrant, 1 to 2 minutes.
  2. Stir kimchi and gochujang into meat mixture and cook until warmed through and fragrant, 2 to 4 minutes. Add rice; cook and stir until rice is heated through and coated with gochujang, 3 to 5 minutes more.
  3. Drizzle sesame oil over rice mixture and stir to coat. Garnish with green parts of green onion.
  4. Melt 1 teaspoon butter in a skillet over medium-high heat. Crack egg into the pan and cook until white is completely set and yolk is thick, 3 to 4 minutes. Place egg over fried rice.
